The debate over which frontier Large Language Model (LLM) reigns supreme for software development has intensified, with Anthropic's Fable and OpenAI's GPT-5.6 Soul drawing strong, often contrasting, opinions from the tech community. A recent in-depth analysis by a leading tech analyst, based on extensive daily usage, outlines critical distinctions in their performance, cost, and developer experience.

The analysis highlights a significant community split: while figures like SolidJS creator Ryan Carneato laud Fable for its uncanny ability to identify issues without explicit prompting and superior design capabilities, others, including Open Code CEO Jay and the analyst's podcast co-host Ben Davis, express a preference for Soul due to its robust persistence and efficiency. The analyst ultimately favors Fable for producing merge-ready code, crafting superior designs, tackling complex problems, and simplifying existing work. Fable excels in understanding developer intent, generates less extraneous code, and demonstrates a 'cleverness' in problem-solving. However, it comes with a higher token cost and slower execution, burning significantly more tokens per task (e.g., 33k vs. Soul's 15k on max settings). Fable also faces criticism for aggressive model rerouting to less capable models and restrictive subscription terms, including its impending removal from current subscription plans.

Conversely, Soul is lauded for its cost-effectiveness and unparalleled token efficiency, often completing tasks in minutes compared to Fable's longer durations. Its diligence, eagerness, and best-in-class computer use capabilities make it ideal for long-running tasks, automation, and quick system fixes. Soul’s strict adherence to instructions, even if potentially destructive (as evidenced by incidents involving unintended file deletions), contrasts with Fable's intent-driven approach. While Soul is adept at fitting into existing codebases and orchestrating sub-agents, its output for novel UI designs is described as aesthetically poor, and it often generates an excessive amount of code. The analyst recommends starting with Soul due to its exceptional value proposition and flexibility across various tools and harnesses, suggesting that its $100 Codeex subscription tier is a fundamental tool for every developer's arsenal before exploring alternatives like Fable.
