Max, a renowned developer and educator recognized for his extensive programming courses, offers a candid and nuanced perspective on Artificial Intelligence, articulating a deep-seated "two hearts pounding" sentiment. While openly bullish on AI's practical applications, he underscores its growing indispensability for developers. Max emphasizes AI tools like Claude Code and Codex as critical enablers for significant productivity boosts, asserting their necessity for both securing and retaining employment in the rapidly evolving tech landscape. He illustrates AI's diverse utility with real-world examples, ranging from automating minor system configurations, such as inverting a mouse wheel, to substantially aiding in major projects like the relaunch of academind.com, Build My Graphic, Max Draw, and the development of various internal tools. For Max, leveraging AI is no longer optional but a professional imperative for efficiency and maintaining competitive advantage.

Despite fully embracing AI for its enabling capabilities, Max openly acknowledges the "depressing aspects and moments" it introduces, particularly the erosion of the intrinsic joy derived from the craft of coding. He describes a profound shift where the deeply satisfying experience of problem-solving through hands-on code creation—achieving a developer's "flow state"—is increasingly supplanted by higher-level tasks. These include writing and reviewing specifications, steering AI, correcting its outputs, and meticulously drafting explicit test cases for generated code. This pivot, he notes, often diverts developers from what he considers the truly enjoyable parts of development. While conceding AI's irreversible presence and the professional necessity to adapt by mastering these tools, Max articulates a personal struggle to reconcile the diminished joy of creation with the undeniable professional benefits, advocating for a continued, strong focus on fundamental programming principles alongside advanced AI tool mastery.
