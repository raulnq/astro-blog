MiduD recently hosted a dynamic 'Tu Web a Juicio' live stream, a community event dedicated to the critical review of developer portfolios and web projects. Despite recurring internet connectivity challenges that tested the host's composure, the session proceeded, leveraging Google's cutting-edge Cloud Fable 5 AI model for real-time analysis. Participants submitted their projects via a designated Discord channel, with strict guidelines emphasizing functional links, repository access, and adherence to content policies, underlining the event's professional and constructive nature.

Cloud Fable 5, guided by specific prompts covering design, UX, web performance, accessibility, SEO, and code quality, demonstrated its analytical prowess. While the AI successfully identified critical issues like incorrect `lang` attributes, poor metadata, and a glaring `<a>` element nested within a `<button>` (a violation of HTML specifications), its attempts at creative design recreation often fell short, producing generic or 'boring' layouts compared to original, more artistic submissions like SU's portfolio. Midu's manual critiques frequently complemented the AI's findings, highlighting issues such as cluttered interfaces, inconsistent visual hierarchies, lack of clear calls-to-action (CTAs), and the common mistake of presenting outdated professional experience first. A standout moment was Cloud Fable's exceptionally scathing yet precise code review of a 'Clean Architecture' Node.js repository, which, despite good architectural intent, was found to have 'severe bugs' and 'leaky abstractions' indicative of insufficient testing.

The session underscored the value of peer review and advanced tooling in software development. Three projects were ultimately recognized for their quality and originality, earning .com or .dev domains sponsored by Don Dominio: SU for its unique and engaging portfolio, Thumbnail Grabber for its practical utility, and Bruno Esparza for his impressive scroll animations. Don Dominio also offered a special 24-hour discount on .com and .dev domains using the code `Midu24H`, further fostering community development. The event confirmed the evolving role of AI as a powerful diagnostic tool, albeit one still maturing in creative design applications.
