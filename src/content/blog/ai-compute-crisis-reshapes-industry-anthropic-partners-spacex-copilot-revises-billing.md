The global compute shortage has reached a critical juncture, compelling major AI labs to forge unexpected alliances and overhaul long-standing product offerings. Anthropic, a leader in AI safety, has reportedly partnered with Elon Musk's SpaceX/XAI to secure crucial GPU capacity. This alliance is particularly notable given their historical antagonism, underscoring the extreme demand for compute resources. Anthropic, which experienced an "80x growth" this year against a projected "10x," will leverage SpaceX's Colossus 1 supercomputer, previously underutilized for Grok inference. This immediate influx of compute has enabled Anthropic to significantly increase Claude's usage limits, including doubling Claude Code's 5-hour rate limits and massively boosting API rate limits for enterprise clients. However, the unchanged weekly limits for Claude Code suggest that compute remains a tight constraint for continuous, heavy users.

In parallel, GitHub Copilot is transitioning its billing model from message-based to token consumption, effective June 1st. This shift addresses what many in the developer community, including prominent figures like Theo, describe as an "abusable loophole" in the previous message-based system. Agentic AI workflows can incur costs orders of magnitude higher than a simple message, making a $40 subscription potentially worth tens of thousands in compute. The move, while causing user dissatisfaction, is framed as a necessary measure to manage severe compute scarcity, rather than a "rug pull." New Copilot sign-ups are temporarily halted, further indicating the intensity of the compute crunch impacting even large players like Microsoft.

Beyond pricing, the proliferation of AI coding agents is sparking industry-wide debate on "cognitive debt" and "skill atrophy." While tools like Claude Code and GitHub Copilot offer unprecedented productivity gains for experienced developers, concerns are mounting that over-reliance on AI for code generation and debugging could diminish fundamental coding skills, particularly among junior engineers. Experts suggest a disciplined approach, leveraging AI for learning and tedious tasks, while consciously engaging with core problem-solving to prevent "guaranteeing their own obsolescence." The shifting landscape demands not only adaptability to new billing models and tool capabilities but also a critical re-evaluation of how developers maintain and evolve their core competencies.
