Anthropic's cutting-edge AI model, Claude Fabel (a variant of the even more powerful Claude Mythos), has been unexpectedly blocked by the United States government for all non-US citizens. Launched on June 9th under Anthropic's highly restrictive Glasswing project, Fabel was hailed as the most potent AI model developed to date, renowned for its capability to identify security vulnerabilities across vast codebases. Access was initially limited to select, top-tier technology partners like AWS, Nvidia, and Microsoft, precisely due to Anthropic's own concerns about its potential misuse, particularly in cybersecurity and biological domains. The abrupt ban, effective immediately, prevents foreign users from accessing Fabel 5 and its foundational Mythos counterpart, though other Anthropic models like Opus 4.8 remain unaffected. The model was originally slated for broader access until June 22nd. 

While an official statement from Anthropic reportedly lacks specific details regarding the government's decision, the consensus points to state security concerns. It is speculated that the government believes Fabel's extensive security barriers, which included automatic session reviews, redirection to less powerful models for sensitive queries (e.g., hacking, bio-weapon development), and anti-distillation measures, may have been circumvented or 'jailbroken.' A significant underlying concern is the potential for model distillation, where advanced models like Fabel could be used by foreign competitors, particularly from China (e.g., Minimx, Moonshoot, Deep Seek), to train and enhance their own AI systems, thereby rapidly closing the technological gap. Anthropic had previously implemented safeguards to prevent such distillation, redirecting users attempting to train other models with Fabel to Opus 4.8. Despite these precautions, the government's action suggests these measures were deemed insufficient. Reports indicate Anthropic may not be in agreement with the government's mandate, hinting at further information expected soon. The sudden blocking has even reportedly impacted prominent AI figures like Andrej Karpathy, underscoring the severity of the government's intervention.
