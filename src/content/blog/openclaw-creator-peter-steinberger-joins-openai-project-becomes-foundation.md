Peter Steinberger, the visionary creator behind the rapidly growing personal AI agent OpenClaw (formerly Cloudbot), has announced his move to OpenAI, where he will lead efforts in developing the next generation of intelligent agents. Simultaneously, OpenClaw is set to transition into an independent open-source foundation, benefiting from continued financial and operational support from OpenAI. This pivotal development signals a dynamic shift in the AI ecosystem, driven by OpenClaw's meteoric rise to become one of the fastest-growing GitHub projects, surpassing established frameworks like Next.js in star count within a month.

Steinberger's decision, as outlined in his announcement, prioritizes stability, freedom to build, and direct access to frontier AI research and models over financial incentives, given his prior startup success. The choice to align with OpenAI, rather than other industry giants like Anthropic or Meta, was influenced by stark philosophical differences. OpenAI has consistently demonstrated a commitment to open-source initiatives, exemplified by its Apache-licensed Codex CLI and support for external harnesses, fostering a collaborative environment. In contrast, Anthropic has historically adopted more restrictive practices, including numerous DMCA takedown requests for inadvertently published source maps and imposing bans on users accessing their models outside proprietary interfaces. This restrictive approach notably led to Anthropic's legal action against Steinberger over the original 'Cloudbot' name and domains. For Steinberger, OpenAI's environment offers the necessary infrastructure and legal stability to focus on his core mission: building an accessible, user-friendly AI agent for everyone, without the overheads of running a large company.
